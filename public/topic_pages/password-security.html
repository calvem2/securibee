about

Online sites use passwords to verify users’ identities and give them access to their accounts. As such, passwords should be strong and kept secret.

what do we mean by a “strong" password?

There are a variety of ways that an adversary might try to learn your password. A strong password can help you minimize the risks of attacks that aim to do so.

how to make a strong password
  • length: 12 or more characters
  • mixed use of upper/lower case letters, numbers, symbols
  • make it hard to guess! Do you use your dog's name or your birthday in your password? 'Piper1999!' may check a lot of boxes, but it's easily guessable!

sources: security.org, WeLiveSecurity

password practices to avoid
  • predictable password patterns that make passwords easy to guess
    • sticking a common symbol at the end (~, !, @, #, $, %, &, ?)
    • sticking a number at the end
    • capitalizing the first letter
  • including guessable or personal information
    • e.g. dates, id numbers, names, places, hobbies
    • this info can be easily guessed or obtained through means like social media
  • repeating letters
  • common keyboard sequences (e.g. qwerty, asdf, zxcvb, 123456)
  • using the same password for multiple accounts - if the password for one of your accounts is compromised, then all of your accounts are
  • using the same pattern to generate passwords (e.g. fac-ix9!-3A for Facebook, twi-ix9!-3A for Twitter, etc.) - if one of these passwords is known, all others can easily be guessed
  • using any variation of 'password' or other common passwords (e.g. P@s$W0rD)

sources: security.org, WeLiveSecurity, Lifehacker

Why you should care

Your online accounts often have personal information that you don’t want anyone else to access. Strong password security can help you avoid the consequences of someone hacking your account.

What could happen if your password isn’t secure? Some examples...

  • online banking: adversaries could transfer money, make purchases, link your account to unwanted services, lock you out of your accounts
  • social media: adversaries could impersonate you, post things you wouldn’t, lock you out of your account, access private messages
  • online shopping: adversaries could access credit card information, make unauthorized purchases, see personal information (name, birthday, email, addresses)
  • google: adversaries could access photos, documents, email, linked accounts (YouTube, Spotify, etc.)

LastPass

assignment

Remembering lots of strong passwords can be hard. Password managers like LastPass can help.

Password managers store your passwords for you, and you can access them using a master password. This way, you only have to remember one password - just make sure that it’s strong!

Sign up for LastPass here.

have i been pwned?

warning

Have I Been Pwned? provides a personal data leak checker can help you find out if your email or password has appeared in a data breach.

This tool will check your email against 10 billion known leaked emails or your password against 613 million leaked passwords.

** Please don't enter a password you currently use into any third-party service like this! The purpose of the tool is to help people who have been using bad password practices to verify that they should not be using those bad password(s) anymore.

** Even if your email or password is not found in any data leaks, it could still be leaked and the tool just doesn't know about it yet!

Access the leak checker here.

Two Factor Authentication

done_all

Also known as 2FA, two-step verification, dual-factor authentication.

Adds an additional layer of security to your accounts by requiring you to verify your identity in two ways. The first ‘factor’ in 2FA is usually your password. The second ‘factor’ could things like be a code sent to your email or phone or a verification request sent to another one of your devices.

You can check if a site offers two factor authentication by going to your account settings.

Some popular sites that offer two factor authentication: Amazon, Apple, Dropbox, Google, Facebook, LastPass, PayPal, LinkedIn, Twitter, Yahoo, Instagram
