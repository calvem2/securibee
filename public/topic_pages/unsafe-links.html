Some tools to help protect yourself against unsafe links

about

Often, malicious websites try to steal information or download malicious content onto a users device by tricking them into pressing “unsafe links”.

what do we mean by "unsafe link"?

An unsafe link is a hyper-link to a website that adversaries use to trick people into downloading malicious content or redirect users to websites that they do not mean to visit.

what can this look like on a website?

adversaries tend to disguise unsafe links using the following:

  • download buttons
  • mispelled website names
  • falsely advertised hyperlinks
  • advertisements that look "too good to be true"
what is a "URL"?

URL stands for Uniform Resource Locator. It is essentially the "address" of the website that you are on.

Example:

"https://www.Google.com is Google's URL

sources: ComputerHope.com

what is a "hyperlink"?

A hyperlink (often also referred to as a "link" or "web link") is an icon text or graphic that when pressed redirects to another file or object.

Example:

When you press on the following **blue text: Facebook , your browser will redirect you to Facebook.com

**NOTE: The text may not always be blue, it can also be underlined or present itself as an image or icon.

sources: ComputerHope.com

what do I do if I press on an "unsafe link"?

Take the following steps

  • Close out of the redirected window and disconnect your computer and then restart it. This is the quickest way to stop any downloads to your computer
  • When your computer restarts, check your downloads for any files that you did not authorize for download. If you do find files, immediately delete and do not click on them.
  • Close all of the applications open on your laptop, ESPECIALLY your browser to make sure it does not try to visit the website again.

Why you should care

Pressing on unsafe links can lead to accidentally downloading a virus or other bad programs onto your computer or accidentally purchasing something you did not mean to buy.

Tools & Tips

We gathered some tips and tools to help you recognize and protect yourself against unsafe links!

Tip #1: Never directly click on a hyperlink on a website you are unfamiliar with.

Instead, inspect the URL that the hyperlinked text or image leads to and make sure that it is the website you want to visit.

Use tools like ScanURL.net , a community sourced website thatasks users to input a url, a website name and a description of the user’s hesitations about the website and will report to users if it has been reported as phishing, potentially dangerous or suspicious in the past.

Tip #2: Check for mispelled organization names

Potential adversaries often misspell common organization website names to try and trick users into clicking on their links.

Examples

  • facbook instead of Facebook
  • am.azon instead of amazon
  • yuotube instead of youtube

Tip #3: Avoid pressing on advertisements that seem "too good to be true"

This is a trick adversaries use to lure people into clicking on their links. If you see an advertisement that looks cheaply made or unrealistic, do not press on it.

Examples

  • "$99 Rayban sunglasses for $20"
  • "Entire store 90% off!"
  • insert example

Tip #4: When downloading a file from a website, make sure that there are no duplicate download buttons.

Sometimes, advesaries insert images onto websites that LOOK like download buttons to trick users into downloading malicious files or to redirect them to websites they do not mean to visit. Double check that you are pressing on the correct download button for the file you wish yto download.
