About

The tech companies that provide things like email, social media, video streaming, etc are “free,” but only because they are able to make profit off of other things such as ads. In other words, these platforms run ads for various companies, and they make the most profit if users click on the ads or otherwise keep using their apps and potentially click on some other ads later on. That is the only way that many companies are able to offer its platforms without requiring users to pay them directly.

This includes the big tech companies like Amazon, Apple, Facebook, Google, Microsoft, and Twitter, as well as all of the services they provide and smaller companies that they own, including but not limited to:

  • Amazon Alexa, Amazon Prime, Amazon Prime Video, Amazon Music, Amazon Web Services, Audible, IMDb, Kindle, Ring, Twitch, Whole Foods, and Zappos
  • Apple
  • Facebook, Facebook Lite, Instagram, Messenger, Portal, and WhatsApp
  • Google Assistant, Google Search, Contacts, Chrome, Gmail, YouTube, Maps, Gmail, Photos, Pixel
  • Hulu
  • Microsoft
  • Netflix
  • Twitter

Why you should care

Using all of the information that a company itself and any partnered companies have about you, and their other users, a company builds profiles about who they think you are and what you might be interested in, and runs ads accordingly.

This might seem to harmlessly personalize the ads and content you see to things that may be more interesting to you, but because companies profit from you staying engaged, they will try to show the content they think will change your behavior towards clicking on the ads – for anything from shopping to voting – and keep you on your device for as long as possible

This has led to deeply disturbing and globally significant stories, such as...

  • Radicalizing YouTube Watchers
  • Disturbing YouTube videos that are tricking children
  • Facebook and the Cambridge Analytica Scandal, in 3 Paragraphs
  • Instagram is intentionally designed to mimic addictive painkillers
  • Google's algorithm’s history of racism against Black women
  • How advertising manipulates your choices and spending habits
