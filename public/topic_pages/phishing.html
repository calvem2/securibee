n/a

Tip #1: Check the email address of the sender. Does it have an official website signature?

Tip #2: Look for misspellings, bad grammar, or casual wording (if it’s from an official source).

Sometimes email phishers will pose as your friends or family members and send a link. For example, they may send an email with a link that looks like a link to YouTube that says “Hey! Check out this hilarious video I just found.” Think to yourself, is this normal behavior for this person to send me emails or is the wording not how they usually sound? It never hurts to reach out and ask the person if it was really them that sent the link or not. If they did not send the link tell them to update their email account password so this doesn’t happen to anyone else in their contacts list and have them contact everyone in their contacts list to let them know to not click the link in any emails sent from their account.

Tip #3: If you do ever come across a phishing email that does not go directly to spam, you can filter other emails sent from that account into your Spam folder so that the next time they try to email you, it won’t go into your inbox.
