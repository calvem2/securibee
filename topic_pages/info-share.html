About

In general, if you are not sure who someone is or do not trust someone, avoid sharing any of this information:

  • Names
  • Social Security Numbers
  • Addresses
  • Email
  • Bank Account Numbers
  • Phone Numbers
  • The content of the text you just recieved
  • Information that is commonly used for security questions, such as your maiden name, first petâ€™s name, what you wanted to be when you grew up, etc

Why you should care

Other people can use these types of information to do things like get access to your bank accounts; get new credit cards or loans under your name; steal your identity; hack into your phone, email, or other accounts; lock you out of your accounts; and more.

These situations can not only be difficult and take a long time to fix, but also give people access to more than you realize, like if you use one password for multiple accounts or store information about one account on another. One possible risk is hurting your credit score, which can hurt your current and future loans or housing situations. It can also potentially put the physical security of you and your loved ones at risk.
