Some tools to help you navigate smart speakers safely

about

Smart speakers have become very popular home technology. They are speakers that use a "wake phrase" to activate voice recognition technology to play music, turn on/off devices in your home, set alarms, make calls, and more!

wwhat are examples of smart speakers?

Some popular examples are Amazon’s “Echo, Google’s “Google Home” and Apple’s “HomePod”.

what is a "wake phrase"?

A "wake phrase" is the phrase that the smart speaker listens for to initiate interaction and listen for a command.

The wake phrases for popular smart speakers are:

  • For the Amazon Echo: "Alexa," "Amazon", "Echo" "Hey, Alexa"
  • For the Google Home: "Okay, Google" or "Hey, Google"
  • For the Apple HomePod : "Okay, Siri" , "Hey, Siri"

sources: DigitalTrends.com

what is voice recognition and how does it work?

Voice recognition is the software that smart speakers use to listen for and execute a given command.

Voice recognition technology works by first analyzing the sounds that the smart device hears, then filtering the sound and converting the sounds to a format that the device understands and then analyzing the converted file for meaning.

Sometimes the voice recognition technology can be trained to be used only by one person, and it will adjust its analysis based on how the user talks. To learn more about how to do this for your device, visit the following sources:

  • "How to train your Amazon Echo to recognize your voice" (source)
  • "Voice Match and media on Google Nest or Home devices" (source)
  • "Use multiuser on HomePod" * (source)

sources: GlobalMe.net

*NOTE: The HomePod is designed to only be used by one user, so the article linked will teach you how to set up voice recognition for multiple users in the case that there is more than one person living in your household.

is the smart speaker always listening?

Smart speakers are always listening for their wake phrase. Many tech companies have modified their privacy agreements for smart speakers to account for accidental recordings and wake phrase triggers.

sources: ConsumerReports.org

Why you should care

Many of these smart speakers use generic wake words to activate their systems, such as “Hey Siri” or “Alexa” or “Hey Google” and by design, these smart speakers are ALWAYS listening (unless instructed otherwise).

Someone could potentially take advantage of this and command your smart speaker to make a purchase, or control devices in your home.

Tools & Tips

We gathered some tips and tools to help you navigate your smart speaker safely!

Tip #1: Make use of the “mute” feature on your smart speaker when you do not want to be heard.

extension

Muting your device means that even when you say the wake phrase, your device will activate. As long as a device is on mute, it will not listen for commands.

To make sure that you are not accidentally waking your smart speaker and allowing it to listen to you, “mute” the device when you do not mean to use it.

Tip #2: Train your device to only listen to the voices of folx in your household.

assignment

Use voice recognition to make your device harder to manipulate for those living outside of your household

Tip #3: Delete your command history on your device and adjust your device's privacy settings

warning

Many times when you have a smart speaker, it will ask you for permission to forward recordings of your voice commands to send to its respective company’s database for use of future voice recognition training. To further protect yourself, mark no for this option
